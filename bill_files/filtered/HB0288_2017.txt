         SCHOOL SUNSCREEN PROVISION           GENERAL SESSION      STATE OF UTAH      Chief Sponsor   Craig Hall      Senate Sponsor                            LONG TITLE      General Description            This bill permits a student to carry and use sunscreen at a public school.       Highlighted Provisions             This bill                   defines sunscreen                   requires a public school to permit a student to possess and use sunscreen at school                   permits a school employee to apply sunscreen on a student under certain conditions        and                  provides immunity for an employee who applies sunscreen on a student and       provides immunity for the employee s employer.       Money Appropriated in this Bill             None       Other Special Clauses             None       Utah Code Sections Affected        ENACTS               A       , Utah Code Annotated                    Be it enacted by the Legislature of the state of Utah             Section  .  Section   A        is enacted to read               A       . Sunscreen    Possession    Self administration.                 As used in this section,  sunscreen  means a compound topically applied to prevent       sunburn.                 A public school shall permit a student, without a parent or physician s       authorization, to possess or self apply sunscreen that is              a   approved by the Food and Drug Administration  and             b   not in an aerosol container.                 If a student is unable to self apply sunscreen, a volunteer school employee may       apply the sunscreen on the student if the student s parent or legal guardian provides written       consent for the assistance.                 A volunteer school employee who applies sunscreen on a student in compliance       with Subsection     and the volunteer school employee s employer are not liable for              a   an adverse reaction suffered by the student as a result of having the sunscreen       applied  or             b   discontinuing the application of the sunscreen at any time.Legislative Review NoteOffice of Legislative Research and General Counsel
