A chief law enforcement officer may not make a certification under this section that       the chief law enforcement officer knows to be untrue.  The chief law enforcement officer may       not refuse to provide certification based on a generalized objection to private persons or entities       making, possessing, or receiving firearms or any certain type of firearm, the possession of       which is not prohibited by law.                 Upon receiving a federal firearm transfer form a chief law enforcement officer or       the chief law enforcement officer s designee shall provide certification if the applicant              a   is not prohibited by law from receiving or possessing the firearm  or             b   is not the subject of a proceeding that could result in the applicant being prohibited       by law from receiving or possessing the firearm.                 The chief law enforcement officer, the chief law enforcement officer s designee, or       official signing the federal transfer form shall              a   return the federal transfer form to the applicant within    calendar days  or             b   if the applicant is denied, provide to the applicant the reasons for denial in writing       within    calendar days.                 Chief law enforcement officers and their employees who act in good faith when       acting within the scope of their duties are immune from liability arising from any act or       omission in making a certification as required by this section.  Any action taken against a chief       law enforcement officer or an employee shall be in accordance with Title   G, Chapter  ,       Governmental Immunity Act of Utah.                 A chief law enforcement officer who receives a certification or notification shall       destroy and delete the certification or notification and any other record that contains       information obtained from the certification or notification within    days after the day on       which the chief law enforcement officer receives the certification or notification.                 A certification or notification and any other record or portion of a record that       contains information gathered from the certification or notification is classified as a private       record in accordance with Title   G, Chapter  , Government Records Access and Management       Act.Legislative Review NoteOffice of Legislative Research and General Counsel
